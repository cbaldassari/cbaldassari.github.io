About

I am a researcher at the Department of Economics, Engineering, Society and Business (DEIM) of the University of Tuscia in Viterbo, and a collaborator at the Rome Technopole Data Lab. I hold a degree in Physics from Sapienza University of Rome.

My research focuses on machine learning, complex networks, signal processing and data mining, with applications to financial and energy markets. I develop unsupervised methodologies that bridge time series analysis and graph theory — from visibility graphs to Wasserstein barycenters.

As a freelance data scientist I have worked on customer churn prediction, marketing analytics, content optimization and data acquisition systems. Outside of research, I am a filmmaker with over 50 short films and documentaries.

Information-Driven Modeling of Energy Markets: An Unbalanced Wasserstein Barycenter Approach

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Applied Stochastic Models in Business and Industry, Wiley

A novel methodology is proposed for jointly modeling the price dynamics of natural gas and electricity by integrating graph-based machine learning and optimal transport theory. The procedure reveals an asymmetric balance between the two markets, with natural gas exerting a structurally dominant influence. A Gaussian Mixture Model is fitted to the unbalanced Wasserstein barycenter using maximum likelihood estimation via the EM algorithm. Applied to Italian market data from 2019 to 2023, a period marked by extreme volatility and systemic shocks, the methodology accurately reproduces both common dynamics and idiosyncratic deviations.

Group Detection in Energy Commodity Markets Through Manifold-Informed Wasserstein Barycenter

Mari C., Laureti T., Baldassari C.

Quality & Quantity, vol. 59, no. 3, pp. 2197–2227

An unsupervised machine learning methodology is proposed to examine connections between energy commodity price dynamics (oil, gas, electricity in the USA) and macroeconomic variables (interest rates and the S&P index). The approach combines three key techniques: the Wasserstein barycenter for identifying optimal probability distributions, Graph Machine Learning for network analysis, and Manifold Learning for dimensionality reduction. Key findings include the discovery of a well-defined group of energy commodity markets that share similar characteristics.

Understanding the Complexities of the Fine Structure of Interest Rates: A Wasserstein Barycenter Learning Approach

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Neural Computing and Applications, vol. 36, pp. 19291–19305

A novel methodology to investigate the fine structure of interest rates based on machine learning techniques, with a fine-tuning of single-maturity interest rates to capture maturity-specific stochastic factors. The proposed analysis tests the hypothesis of a market segmentation into a short-term segment (the money market) and a long-term segment (the capital market), each with its own segment-specific stochastic factors. The methodology was tested on the US zero-coupon Treasury yield curve.

Stochastic Inflation and the Term Structure of Interest Rates: A Simple Diffusion Model

Baldassari C., Meschini M., Nascenzi P.

Journal of Finance and Investment Analysis, vol. 13, no. 4, pp. 1–2

The paper addresses valuing contingent claims on the term structure in a single good economy under uncertain inflation. A simple diffusion model for pricing inflation-indexed securities is proposed in the arbitrage-free context. A martingale characterization of nominal and real prices is given, a stochastic generalization of the Fisher equation is provided, and a two-factor model for practical inflation-linked security valuation is discussed.

A Graph-Based Superframework for Mixture Model Estimation Using EM: An Analysis of US Wholesale Electricity Markets

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Neural Computing and Applications, vol. 35, no. 20, pp. 14867–14883

A fully unsupervised graph-based superframework is proposed to handle the EM initialization problem for estimating mixture models on financial time series. Using a complex network approach that links time series and graphs, graph-structured information derived from the observed data is exploited to produce a meaningful starting point for the EM algorithm. It is shown that structural information derived by complex graphs can definitely capture time series behavior and nonlinear relationships between different observations.

Optimization of Mixture Models on Time Series Networks Encoded by Visibility Graphs: An Analysis of the US Electricity Market

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Computational Management Science, vol. 20, no. 1, article 28

A fully unsupervised network-based methodology for estimating Gaussian Mixture Models on financial time series by maximum likelihood using the EM algorithm, with visibility graph-structured information of observed data used to initialize the algorithm. Encoding time series through Visibility Graphs allows capturing the behavior of the time series and the nonlinear interactions between observations. The methodology is applied to the US wholesale electricity market.

Unsupervised Expectation-Maximization Algorithm Initialization for Mixture Models: A Complex Network-Driven Approach for Modeling Financial Time Series

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Information Sciences, vol. 617, pp. 1–16

The methodology exploits the duality between univariate time series and complex networks to provide a new approach for estimating mixture models by maximum likelihood based on an unsupervised initialization technique of the EM algorithm. A fully unsupervised network-based initialization technique is provided by mapping time series to complex networks using the Markov Transition Field as adjacency matrix. The technique produces significantly better results, outperforming existing methods in terms of maximum likelihood, a lower number of components, and agreement with the empirical distribution of log-returns.

Using Text Data Instead of SIC Codes to Tag Innovative Firms and Classify Industrial Activities

Marra A., Baldassari C.

PLoS ONE, vol. 17, no. 6, e0270041

The study employs text mining and semantic algorithms to classify innovative companies as an alternative to standard industrial classification codes. Rather than relying on SIC codes, information is gathered from company websites and corporate documents, extracting keywords to generate tags about firms' activities, specializations and competencies. By matching firms' keywords, researchers can measure interconnectedness and derive industrial proximity measures. The analysis examined 583 innovative firms in Italy's Chieti-Pescara provinces.

Ensemble Methods for Jump-Diffusion Models of Power Prices

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Energies, vol. 14, no. 8, 2084

A machine learning-based methodology which makes use of ensemble methods for treating missing data in time series with irregular observation times, detecting anomalies and defining suitable models of system dynamics. Applied to US wholesale electricity price time series characterized by missing data, high volatility, jumps and pronounced spikes. The missForest algorithm handles missing data while the isolation forest algorithm detects anomalous regions, allowing suitable jump-diffusion models of power prices to be defined.

Exploring Networks of Proximity for Partner Selection, Firms' Collaboration and Knowledge Exchange. The Case of Clean-Tech Industry

Marra A., Carlei V., Baldassari C.

Business Strategy and the Environment, vol. 29, no. 3, pp. 1034–1044

The paper examines how firms can identify suitable collaboration partners through network analysis within the clean-technology sector. 4,782 clean-tech companies globally are analyzed to develop a methodology supporting partner screening and knowledge exchange. Energy companies pursuing specialization should leverage their network of business proximity, while those seeking diversification should explore their strategic proximity network to engage with firms possessing complementary capabilities.

Marra A., Baldassari C., Cantelmo A.

Digital Technologies for Social Innovation: An Empirical Recognition on the New Enablers

Maiolini R., Marra A., Baldassari C., Carlei V.

Journal of Technology Management and Innovation, vol. 11, no. 4, pp. 22–28

The article offers empirical recognition to Social Innovation by investigating social and innovative activities conducted by startups and SMEs across the world between 2001 and 2014. It captures SI core businesses and underlying complementarities between products, markets and technologies, showing how digital media and IT are tracing innovation trajectories over a multitude of industries, leading current industrial patterns of SI and fostering its cross-industry nature.
