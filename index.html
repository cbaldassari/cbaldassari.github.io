01 / About

Background

I'm a researcher at the University of Tuscia in Viterbo, with a physics degree from Sapienza in Rome. I started out in consulting, working on fraud detection, predictive models and analytics for banks and media companies, then eventually followed my curiosity into academia.

Today I work on unsupervised methods that connect time series and graph theory, mostly applied to energy and financial markets. I like finding structure where it's not obvious there is any.

2026

Information-Driven Modeling of Energy Markets: An Unbalanced Wasserstein Barycenter Approach

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Applied Stochastic Models in Business and Industry, Wiley

A novel methodology is proposed for jointly modeling the price dynamics of natural gas and electricity by integrating graph-based machine learning and optimal transport theory. The procedure reveals an asymmetric balance between the two markets, with natural gas exerting a structurally dominant influence. A Gaussian Mixture Model is fitted to the unbalanced Wasserstein barycenter using maximum likelihood estimation via the EM algorithm. Applied to Italian market data from 2019 to 2023, the methodology accurately reproduces both common dynamics and idiosyncratic deviations.

Group Detection in Energy Commodity Markets Through Manifold-Informed Wasserstein Barycenter

Mari C., Laureti T., Baldassari C.

Quality & Quantity, vol. 59, no. 3, pp. 2197–2227

An unsupervised machine learning methodology to examine connections between energy commodity price dynamics (oil, gas, electricity in the USA) and macroeconomic variables. The approach combines three key techniques: the Wasserstein barycenter for identifying optimal probability distributions, Graph Machine Learning for network analysis, and Manifold Learning for dimensionality reduction.

Understanding the Complexities of the Fine Structure of Interest Rates: A Wasserstein Barycenter Learning Approach

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Neural Computing and Applications, vol. 36, pp. 19291–19305

A novel methodology to investigate the fine structure of interest rates based on machine learning techniques, with fine-tuning of single-maturity interest rates to capture maturity-specific stochastic factors. The analysis tests the hypothesis of market segmentation into a short-term and long-term segment, tested on the US zero-coupon Treasury yield curve.

A Graph-Based Superframework for Mixture Model Estimation Using EM: An Analysis of US Wholesale Electricity Markets

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Neural Computing and Applications, vol. 35, no. 20, pp. 14867–14883

A fully unsupervised graph-based superframework to handle the EM initialization problem for estimating mixture models on financial time series. Structural information derived by complex graphs captures time series behavior and nonlinear relationships between different observations.

Optimization of Mixture Models on Time Series Networks Encoded by Visibility Graphs: An Analysis of the US Electricity Market

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Computational Management Science, vol. 20, no. 1, article 28

A fully unsupervised network-based methodology for estimating Gaussian Mixture Models using the EM algorithm with visibility graph-structured information. Encoding time series through Visibility Graphs captures the behavior and nonlinear interactions between observations.

Unsupervised Expectation-Maximization Algorithm Initialization for Mixture Models: A Complex Network-Driven Approach for Modeling Financial Time Series

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Information Sciences, vol. 617, pp. 1–16

Exploiting the duality between univariate time series and complex networks to provide a new approach for estimating mixture models by maximum likelihood. A fully unsupervised initialization technique maps time series to complex networks using the Markov Transition Field as adjacency matrix, outperforming existing methods.

Using Text Data Instead of SIC Codes to Tag Innovative Firms and Classify Industrial Activities

Marra A., Baldassari C.

PLoS ONE, vol. 17, no. 6, e0270041

Text mining and semantic algorithms to classify innovative companies as an alternative to standard industrial classification codes. Information is gathered from company websites and corporate documents, extracting keywords to derive industrial proximity measures. The analysis examined 583 innovative firms in Italy.

Ensemble Methods for Jump-Diffusion Models of Power Prices

Mari C., Baldassari C.

Energies, vol. 14, no. 8, 2084

A machine learning-based methodology using ensemble methods for treating missing data in time series, detecting anomalies and defining suitable models of system dynamics. Applied to US wholesale electricity prices with missForest for imputation and isolation forest for anomaly detection.
